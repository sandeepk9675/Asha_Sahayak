PRADHAN MANTRI SURAKSHIT MATRITVA ABHIYAN (PMSMA) GUIDELINES
=============================================================

OBJECTIVE:
PMSMA aims to provide assured, comprehensive and quality antenatal care to every pregnant
woman on the 9th of every month. The scheme focuses on early detection and management of
high-risk pregnancies (HRP).

ANTENATAL CARE (ANC) SCHEDULE:
- Minimum 4 ANC visits recommended during pregnancy
- First visit: Within 12 weeks of pregnancy (First Trimester)
- Second visit: Between 14-26 weeks (Second Trimester)
- Third visit: Between 28-34 weeks (Third Trimester)
- Fourth visit: Between 36-40 weeks (Third Trimester)

PMSMA 9TH OF EVERY MONTH VISITS:
- Every pregnant woman should visit the health facility on 9th of every month
- Free checkup including: BP, Weight, Hemoglobin, Urine tests
- Specialist consultation available at designated facilities
- Ultrasound examination where available

MCP CARD (Mother and Child Protection Card):
- Green Sticker: Normal pregnancy, routine ANC
- Red Sticker: High-risk pregnancy, needs extra monitoring

HIGH-RISK PREGNANCY (HRP) IDENTIFICATION:
1. Age less than 18 years or more than 35 years
2. Height less than 145 cm
3. Weight less than 40 kg
4. Hemoglobin less than 7 g/dL (severe anemia)
5. Blood pressure more than 140/90 mmHg
6. Previous cesarean section
7. Previous stillbirth or neonatal death
8. Previous history of pre-eclampsia/eclampsia
9. Gestational diabetes
10. Multiple pregnancy (twins/triplets)
11. Rh-negative blood group
12. Bad obstetric history

e-PMSMA (Electronic PMSMA):
- Name-based tracking of all pregnant women
- Individual HRP tracking with follow-up scheduling
- ASHA incentive tracking support
- Line listing of all high-risk pregnancies

DANGER SIGNS REQUIRING IMMEDIATE REFERRAL:
1. Vaginal bleeding at any time during pregnancy
2. Convulsions or seizures
3. Severe headache with blurred vision
4. High fever (>38°C) with inability to get out of bed
5. Severe abdominal pain
6. Fast or difficult breathing
7. Reduced or absent fetal movements
8. Water breaking before labor
9. Blood pressure >160/110 mmHg (severe pre-eclampsia)
10. Hemoglobin <7 g/dL (severe anemia)

IRON FOLIC ACID (IFA) SUPPLEMENTATION:
- All pregnant women: 1 IFA tablet daily (100mg elemental iron + 500mcg folic acid)
- Start from confirmed pregnancy, continue till 180 days postpartum
- For moderate anemia (Hb 7-10): Double dose IFA
- For severe anemia (Hb <7): Parenteral iron therapy + referral

CALCIUM SUPPLEMENTATION:
- 500mg calcium twice daily from 14 weeks till delivery
- Reduces risk of pre-eclampsia and preterm birth

DEWORMING:
- Single dose Albendazole 400mg after first trimester

TETANUS TOXOID (TT):
- TT-1: Early in pregnancy
- TT-2: One month after TT-1
- TT-Booster: If immunized within last 3 years

BIRTH PREPAREDNESS AND COMPLICATION READINESS:
- Identify delivery facility
- Arrange transport
- Save money for emergencies
- Identify blood donor
- Keep emergency contact numbers handy
