WHO ANTENATAL CARE RECOMMENDATIONS
=====================================

OVERVIEW:
The WHO recommends a minimum of 8 antenatal care (ANC) contacts during pregnancy
to reduce perinatal mortality and improve women's experience of care.

RECOMMENDED ANC CONTACT SCHEDULE:
- Contact 1: Up to 12 weeks
- Contact 2: 20 weeks
- Contact 3: 26 weeks
- Contact 4: 30 weeks
- Contact 5: 34 weeks
- Contact 6: 36 weeks
- Contact 7: 38 weeks
- Contact 8: 40 weeks

NOTE: India's PMSMA follows a minimum 4-visit model adapted for resource settings.

NUTRITIONAL INTERVENTIONS:

A. Dietary Counseling:
- Balanced energy and protein dietary supplementation for undernourished populations
- Caloric increase in pregnancy: +340 kcal/day (2nd trimester), +450 kcal/day (3rd trimester)
- Emphasis on diverse diet: cereals, legumes, fruits, vegetables, dairy, eggs, fish/meat

B. Iron and Folic Acid:
- Daily oral iron: 30-60 mg elemental iron
- Daily folic acid: 400 mcg
- Start as early as possible, continue through pregnancy
- In populations with high anemia prevalence (>40%): 60mg iron recommended

C. Calcium:
- Daily calcium: 1.5-2g (in populations with low calcium intake)
- Reduces risk of pre-eclampsia, preterm birth, and maternal mortality
- From 20 weeks onwards

D. Vitamin A:
- Only in areas with severe vitamin A deficiency (not routinely recommended)
- Maximum dose: 10,000 IU daily or 25,000 IU weekly

E. Zinc, Multiple Micronutrients:
- Zinc: Not routinely recommended
- Multiple micronutrient supplements: May replace IFA in context of rigorous research

MATERNAL ASSESSMENT:

A. Anemia Detection:
- Full blood count recommended at first visit
- Hemoglobin testing at each contact where possible
- Anemia classification:
  * Normal: Hb ≥ 11 g/dL
  * Mild anemia: Hb 10-10.9 g/dL
  * Moderate anemia: Hb 7-9.9 g/dL
  * Severe anemia: Hb < 7 g/dL (EMERGENCY - immediate referral)

B. Gestational Diabetes:
- Hyperglycemia first detected in pregnancy:
  * Diabetes in pregnancy: Fasting glucose ≥ 7.0 mmol/L (126 mg/dL)
  * Gestational diabetes: Fasting glucose 5.1-6.9 mmol/L (92-125 mg/dL)
- OGTT (Oral Glucose Tolerance Test) recommended at 24-28 weeks
- Management: Diet modification first, then insulin if needed

C. Hypertensive Disorders:
- BP measurement at every ANC contact
- Classification:
  * Normal: <120/80 mmHg
  * Elevated: 120-129/<80 mmHg
  * Hypertension Stage 1: 130-139/80-89 mmHg
  * Hypertension Stage 2: ≥140/90 mmHg
  * Hypertensive Crisis: >180/120 mmHg
- Pre-eclampsia: BP ≥140/90 + proteinuria after 20 weeks
- Severe pre-eclampsia: BP ≥160/110 + symptoms (headache, visual disturbances, epigastric pain)
- Eclampsia: Pre-eclampsia + seizures (EMERGENCY)
- Management:
  * Mild: Monitor, low-dose aspirin if risk factors present
  * Severe: Immediate hospitalization, antihypertensive therapy, consider delivery

D. Fetal Assessment:
- Symphysis-fundal height measurement at each visit after 24 weeks
- Fetal heart rate detection (Doppler from 12 weeks, stethoscope from 20 weeks)
- Fetal movement counting from 28 weeks
- Ultrasound: At least one scan before 24 weeks (dating, anomaly screening)

PREVENTIVE MEASURES:
1. Tetanus toxoid vaccination
2. Deworming (after first trimester)
3. Malaria prevention in endemic areas (ITN, IPTp)
4. HIV testing (opt-out approach)
5. Syphilis testing and treatment
6. Hepatitis B screening

COUNSELING TOPICS:
1. Nutrition and diet
2. Physical activity (moderate exercise encouraged)
3. Sexual health
4. Danger signs recognition
5. Birth preparedness
6. Breastfeeding preparation
7. Family planning
8. Substance use cessation (tobacco, alcohol)
9. Violence screening
10. Mental health awareness (anxiety, depression screening)

DRUG SAFETY IN PREGNANCY:
Safe: Paracetamol, IFA, calcium, folic acid, methyldopa (for BP), insulin
Avoid: NSAIDs (after 28 weeks), ACE inhibitors, certain antibiotics (tetracycline, aminoglycosides)
Consult: Antidepressants, antiepileptics, anticoagulants
