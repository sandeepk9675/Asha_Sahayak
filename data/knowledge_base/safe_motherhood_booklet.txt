SAFE MOTHERHOOD BOOKLET — KEY GUIDELINES
==========================================

PRENATAL CARE:
1. Register pregnancy within first 12 weeks
2. Minimum 4 ANC visits during pregnancy
3. Take IFA tablets daily as prescribed
4. Take calcium supplements as prescribed
5. Get tetanus toxoid immunization
6. Eat a balanced, nutritious diet
7. Get adequate rest (at least 8 hours sleep)
8. Avoid heavy physical labor
9. Avoid tobacco, alcohol, and harmful substances
10. Report any danger signs immediately

NUTRITION DURING PREGNANCY:
- Eat one extra meal per day during pregnancy
- Include protein-rich foods: dal, eggs, milk, soy, groundnuts
- Include iron-rich foods: green leafy vegetables, jaggery, dates, beetroot
- Include calcium-rich foods: milk, curd, ragi, sesame seeds
- Include fruits and vegetables daily
- Drink plenty of water (8-10 glasses daily)
- Avoid raw or undercooked meat/eggs
- Limit caffeine intake

WEIGHT GAIN DURING PREGNANCY:
- Normal BMI (18.5-24.9): Gain 11.5-16 kg
- Underweight (BMI <18.5): Gain 12.5-18 kg
- Overweight (BMI 25-29.9): Gain 7-11.5 kg
- Obese (BMI ≥30): Gain 5-9 kg

COMMON PREGNANCY DISCOMFORTS AND MANAGEMENT:
1. Morning sickness: Small frequent meals, avoid spicy/oily food, ginger tea
2. Heartburn: Small meals, avoid lying down after eating, elevate head while sleeping
3. Constipation: High fiber diet, plenty of water, regular walking
4. Back pain: Good posture, comfortable shoes, gentle exercises
5. Leg cramps: Stretching, calcium supplementation, hydration
6. Swelling of feet: Elevate feet, reduce salt, rest with feet up

BIRTH PREPAREDNESS PLAN:
1. Select delivery facility (minimum PHC for normal, CHC/DH for high-risk)
2. Identify skilled birth attendant
3. Arrange transportation (108 ambulance number)
4. Save money (minimal Rs 3000-5000 for emergencies)
5. Identify compatible blood donor
6. Pack delivery kit: clean cloth, blade, cord tie, sanitary pads
7. Keep important documents ready: MCP card, Aadhaar, JSSK card

POSTNATAL CARE:
- Stay at facility for minimum 48 hours after delivery
- Initiate breastfeeding within 1 hour of birth
- Exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months
- IFA supplementation continues for 180 days after delivery
- 3 postnatal visits: Day 3, Day 7, and Week 6
- Watch for: excessive bleeding, fever, foul-smelling discharge, breast problems

FAMILY PLANNING COUNSELING:
- Discuss family planning options during ANC visits
- Postpartum contraception options
- Birth spacing: minimum 2 years between pregnancies
